    • Labs

    Every single one of your labs must be submitted with two written text files, RUNS.txt and README.txt. Your README.txt is a text file that will describe your program. Write it as if you are as addressing a technical manager whom you want to understand what you have done, the alternatives that you had, why you made the choices you made, and how you tested your program. Your RUNS.txt should be a document describing the data you have used to test your program and what you have learned from it.

    Students have the option of demonstrating their lab to the lab instructor (me). I strongly suggest you do this. It will be much easier to give credit for your work if you can explain what you have done. Students should email me by the due date to express their intention to demo the lab and we will arrange a time typically within one week for the demonstration. Your demonstration can also occur during lab time or during office hours, permitting other students do not have questions or need help with the current assignment.

    Honor Code

    The usual standards of academic honesty apply to this lab. While you are encouraged to discuss ideas with other students, what you turn in should be your own work. Any substantial help you have received from a person (e.g., fellow student, friend, relative, etc.) or other resource (e.g., website, supplementary textbook, etc.) should cited in your submission. Uncredited assistance will be considered a violation of the honor code.

    The following are definite violations of the honor code

    • Looking at another student's answers
    • Showing another student your answers
    • Turning in work that is clearly not original, e.g., two students turning in virtually identical answers

    It bears repeating that the work you turn in is to be your own.
